Before You Begin

AP Calculus BC — Practice Exam

The AP Calculus BC exam is a 3 hr 15 min hybrid digital test with two equal-weight sections, split into calculator-allowed and calculator-restricted parts.

3 hr 15 min total 45 multiple choice 6 free response
Section Breakdown
Section I — Multiple Choice
50% of score · 45 questions · 1 hr 45 min
Part A 33 questions · 62 minNo Calc
Part B 12 questions · 38 minCalculator
Section II — Free Response
50% of score · 6 questions · 1 hr 30 min
Part A 2 questions · 30 minCalculator
Part B 4 questions · 60 minNo Calc

All FRQs include at least two with real-world contexts. Each free-response question is worth 9 points total.

Calculator Policy
  • Only College Board-approved graphing calculators are allowed in calculator-permitted parts.
  • No calculators of any kind in the non-calculator parts.
  • Check the official AP Exams Calculator Policy before test day.
Test-Day Procedures
  1. 1Arrival: Be at your test center early; ID and AP Student ID required.
  2. 2Check-in: Sign in to Bluebook; receive a paper free-response booklet.
  3. 3Multiple Choice: Complete in Bluebook; no paper work unless permitted.
  4. 4Free Response: View prompts in Bluebook, write answers by hand in the booklet.
  5. 5Break: Scheduled between sections; follow center rules.
  6. 6Submission: Booklet collected for scoring; no personal copies allowed.
Key Tips for Test Day
  • Bring your approved calculator if the part allows it.
  • Manage time — each section is timed; plan your answers to fit.
  • Follow notation — show work clearly for partial credit.
  • Use real-world context — read carefully to avoid misinterpretation.
  • No personal items in the testing area; follow all center rules.
How This Practice Exam Works
  • Multiple-choice questions are graded automatically as soon as you submit.
  • Free-response questions are self-graded: you'll answer on paper, then click Reveal Answer to see the model solution and rubric, and enter how many of the 9 points you earned.
  • Each FRQ's 9 points break down as Part (a): 2, Part (b): 2, Part (c): 2, Part (d): 3.
  • You'll get a 1-minute break after Part B of each section, plus a 10-minute break between Section I and Section II — just like the real exam.
  • Once a part's timer runs out, you'll be moved on automatically with whatever you've answered so far.
Ready when you are — this exam takes about 3 hr 15 min uninterrupted.
